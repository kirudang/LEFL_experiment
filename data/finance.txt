Understanding Finance Law.

Finance law regulates the management of money, banking, investments, and financial transactions. It ensures stability and fairness in:
	1.	Banking – Rules for how banks operate and serve customers.
	2.	Securities – Regulations for stocks, bonds, and financial markets.
	3.	Consumer Protection – Laws that prevent fraud and ensure fair lending.

Key terms include:
- Interest Rate: The cost of borrowing money.
- Securities: Tradable financial assets like stocks or bonds.
- Regulation: Government rules that oversee financial institutions.

These laws help maintain trust in the financial system and protect individuals and businesses alike.
